Interaction Design Analysis

Click on the buttons below to see the two sites I chose to analyze

7 Poems Orb.Farm

Information Design

The first website that I found interesting was 7 Poems. Its interface is super original, very different from what we usually see. There are no clear instructions, and the structure is strange, which encourages us to explore the page on our own. You quickly understand that you have to click on the rectangles to make the poems appear, word by word with a sound effect for each word. The sound stops when the poem stops. This web-site is more focused on atmosphere and poetry than on understanding. In contrast, Orb.Farm is more logical. There are no explanations either, but it’s easier to figure out what to do. In the center, there is an empty aquarium that you can fill with the elements at the top right. It’s an almost scientific site, where the elements interact naturally. You can build a small ecosystem and watch how it works. Both of these sites are very contemplative. You interact by guessing what to do. 7 Poems is more artistic and mysterious, while Orb.Farm is clearer and easier to understand thanks to its interface.

Mapping

The mapping in 7 Poems is kind of unusual. When you click, things happen that you don’t really expect. That makes the experience more surprising and poetic. You can read the poems, but you have to really focus and keep your eyes on the screen. In Orb.Farm, the mapping is much simpler. For example, if you click on “sand,” it adds sand, if you click on “fish,” it adds a fish. The actions are logical and easy to understand. While Orb.Farm is all about logic, 7 Poems is more artistic and personal.

Characterization

The interface of 7 Poems is very simple, with only two colors. It creates a mysterious feeling, like reading someone’s secret diary. Orb.Farm has a colder and less personal interface. The pixel art style makes it feel more like a video game, so the website feels more usual. It really shows how the interface can completely change the way we see and feel a website.

Feedback

In 7 Poems, the feedback is slow and calm. It makes the atmosphere peaceful. In Orb.Farm, the animations are fast and full of energy. The elements fall quickly when you place them and keep moving. It’s a continuous animation that makes the site feel more alive and immersive. The difference in feedback shows how movement and timing can completely change how we feel when we interact with a website.

To conclude

Both website really attracted me with their ui, even if they’re really different, they both show how ui design is important and how mapping, interface, and feedback shape the aesthetic and the experience of the website witch I found amazing. 7Poems made me feel like in was entering in someone secret garden, it was calm and peaceful, Orb.Farm made me feel like I was playing a game but also experiencing a scientific experience. Two completely different world just as interesting !
